OUR INITIATIVES

Scheme for Her Empowerment

SHE CODERS

An online group under our prominent alumna Ms. Daina Emmanuel, the purpose of which is to make the girls industrially ready by helping them with learning a programming language like C++ or Python and becoming technically proficient. We have about 80 girls, all with a strong will to become better Coders under the SHE Coders group. To simplify the learning process, the girls have been divided into a few 15 to 16 groups with 2 peer mentors in each group and the girls post the codes in their GitHub accounts after learning them. We also have subgroups with the cause of teaching the basics of programming. We share the video tutorials through our youtube channel for their better understanding. We hope to continue this learning process in an active manner in the future too and reach out to everyone in need of this mentoring.

ONLINE COURSE

An online course on AI was undertaken by 16 of our girls under the guidance of Prof. K Sudhakar, a retired IIT Professor on Coursera platform. The 4 weeks of learning taught the girls how to be committed to work and how to do away with laziness. Getting inspired by Pro.Sudhakar Sir’s personality, a few girls mainly the final year girls took initiative to help and support fellow girls as well as junior girls in learning something new and useful. There are 4 to 5 groups led by the senior girls, the main purpose of which is to work along with everyone and learn together as combined studies or a peer to peer connection have always been proven beneficial. A few groups have successfully completed their first learning task and received their certificates.

MENTORSHIP PROGRAM

The SHE Mentorship program promises to give the girls a clear picture of the wide world they are going to step in and the ways and means to adapt to the work life through live interactions with experts from different fields. Well-known alumni of our college whole - heartedly came forward to illuminate and guide the girls about what lies ahead for us in life once we step out of the college. The Inauguration of this mentorship program was a grand feat, it was done by Dr. Sudha UPV who enlightened the attendees with her life experiences and many of our alumni too joined the inaugural session and expressed their happiness for SHE having come up with such an initiative.There are 8 groups with 2 to 3 mentors in each one. The groups have been divided based on the interests of the students and the responses received from them. The following are the areas included:

1. Business development officers, business strategists, Business analysts
2. Entrepreneurs
3. Government Sector - Civil, EEE/EC, ME, CS, IES, PSC, PSU
4. Researchers, Teachers, Scholars, Higher education in Indian as well as foreign universities
5. Data Scientist/Analyst, Software Developers,
6. Defence sector
7. Marketing and sales, Technical writing, Content development, Documentation specialist
8. Project Managers, Product Managers, product analyst, Test engineer.

LOCKDOWN TALK SERIES

A one of its kind experience by exploiting the wide opportunities provided by virtual platforms. With around 12 talk sessions, the event was such that the girls had some takeaways at the end of each session from clearing of competitive exams to securing jobs in the public sector units and also about the opportunities of studying abroad. We were able to bring in many renowned alumni of our institution for encouraging and guiding the girls to be on the right track after their Engineering education. The proud moment regarding this event was that it marked the esteemed presence of distinguished personalities such as Dr. B N Suresh, Chancellor IIST and Lt Cdr P. Swathi, a female officer in the Indian Navy and we were lucky enough to have had them with us and got all our queries cleared about joining the respective fields they shone. While some talk sessions were more focused on career guidance, other sessions were about general topics like mental health, healthy lifestyle, ideas and inspirations, life experiences sharing, etc. The highlight factor of the lockdown talk series was that it saw great participation from the students’ end. Students were keen enough to volunteer for and moderate the sessions and this helped them learn what it takes to organize and conduct events of this kind.
